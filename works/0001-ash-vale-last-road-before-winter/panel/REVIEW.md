# Panel review: Ash Vale - Last Road Before Winter

## Scope

Reviewed the first REEL video package for a BANISH Games Design scenario.

## Score

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Rhythm | 21/25 | Eight shots create a clear trailer arc. Future Remotion pass should vary shot duration more aggressively for phone/social. |
| Emotion | 22/25 | The package lands the BANISH thesis that loss becomes memory rather than failure. |
| Execution | 18/25 | Strong manifest and smoke-render path; final art, music, and actual animation remain future work. |
| Legibility | 23/25 | Scenario stakes, player verbs, and captions are phone-readable by design. |

## Role findings

| Role | Finding | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Story Director | The trailer preserves the Ash Vale forge choice and Pilgrim Loss loop without inventing new canon. | Pass. |
| Animation Director | `isometric-game` is the right first style for a settlement/route scenario. | Carry `storyboard-animatic` as the fallback. |
| Editor | The shotlist has a complete 60-second shape; the 45-second phone cut should compress shots 003-006. | Add phone-cut timing in a later export pulse. |
| Sound Designer | The sound plan treats wind, carts, forge, and ledger ticks as structural cues. | Keep sparse sound until real animation exists. |
| Platform and Audience | Captions and phone-safe lower thirds are specified throughout. | Pass for first package. |
